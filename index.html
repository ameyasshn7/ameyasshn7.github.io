About Me

I am a PhD student at Oregon State University, advised by Dr. Jing Zhou, where my research centers on advancing Computer Vision (CV) and edge computing for agricultural applications.

Research Focus

My primary research lies at the intersection of computer vision and precision agriculture. I am currently developing systems that automate the detection of off-type seeds—a task traditionally dependent on intensive human labor. By leveraging state-of-the-art deep learning models, my work aims to improve both the accuracy and efficiency of seed sorting. To make these solutions accessible and scalable, I utilize affordable edge computing platforms such as Raspberry Pi and NVIDIA Jetson boards as user interfaces and on-site processing units.

In addition to seed detection, I am exploring robust, off-grid systems capable of running computer vision algorithms in remote agricultural environments where connectivity is limited. This approach enables real-time data processing and decision-making directly on the farm, which is critical for timely interventions in crop management and resource optimization.
